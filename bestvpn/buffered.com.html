Buffered Summary

Buffered is a relatively new VPN company based in Hungary. They are one of the fastest developing VPN companies we’ve seen, with innovations not seen in other providers, alongside a great VPN product. Their software and website is really easy to use and works well. They are highly recommended.

Pricing & Plans

Like with most other providers Buffered offers the same package but with increasing discounts the longer you sign up for. Admittedly the price is higher than average, but their speeds are excellent, their client software is great, and it has some features built in that no other VPN service has. We think it is well worth the price.


Features

The company is based in Hungary which is a big plus, they are away from the jurisdiction of the US and UK authorities, not to mention the EU has recently declared that data retention is not required by ISPs, meaning Hungary is not either.

The company website does seem to be geared a bit more towards freedom and taking back control from governments and corporations, which is a nice touch. Buffered really believe in what they are doing, privacy.

The company definitely gives of the aura of being a startup and we really like the stuff they are working on. One of the features they’ve added is called discovery which at its core helps you break the firewalls of password protected wifi like hotels and companies. On the technical side, it can find open ports and use those as the OpenVPN port, meaning the VPN can potentially bypass any wifi password protected area where they have ports open. Most often this is the DNS port by the way.

We know that Buffered are also working on some other exciting innovations as well.

Not only that but they allow 3 simultaneous connections which is more than most providers out there. Clearly this is because they wish for you to use your VPN as much as you can. They also have servers in 14 countries all around the world, with top level speeds.

Security & Privacy

Buffered understand the need for security and privacy in the modern world and therefore they have done the best they can, as much as the law allows them. The biggest difference we see from other VPN providers is they’ve done away with the insecure PPTP and L2TP and only support OpenVPN. While this is fine for most devices it does have some limitations on older devices (see below).

Along side this they use shared IPs which means that even if they receive a take down request they can’t pinpoint the user.

The website

The Buffered website is nicely laid out, and while it’s still rough around the edges, it is very simple and see through. This makes it easy to find everything from their offers to their privacy policy.

Support

Buffered has a ticketing mechanism which depending on when we tried it had a response time from 20 minutes to 2 hours. There is no live support although they do say that they have plans to implement this.

The Process

Signing Up

Signing up is a seamless process, as with most other providers. We were able to complete our registration and start using the software in under five minutes.

The Windows VPN client

There are client available for all three major PC platforms: Windows, Mac, Linux. We gave the Windows client, which is based on the OpenVPN client, a test.

The client is very simple and supports no bells and whistle (though some to come in the future). Connecting to a server is simple as clicking the relevant flag, and within a few second you’re good to carry out your internet activity securely.

Performance (Speed, DNS and IP Test)

We gave Buffered the usual speed, DNS Leak and IP tests, that we carry out for every other VPN provider. We found no issues with the IP tests or DNS leak at all. We were very happily surprised with the speed tests as these outperformed most providers out there and put them in line with the big dogs.

Other Platforms

As mentioned it is also available for Linux and Mac. However, since they provide the .ovpn config file, you can also use it on any other devices that accepts this. The biggest issue we can think of wit this, is if you are using Android older than 4.x. We gave it a test on our Android 4.3 Phone as well as a tablet and we found the speeds to be in the same order as those we achieved with our computer.

Conclusion

We liked

  • Amazing innovations
  • Great Speed
  • Great Security
  • Based in Hungary (no DRD, DMCA)

We weren’t so sure about

  • Still very early stage
  • No Android 2.x support
  • Quantity of support (quality is great though)

We hated

  • Not much

Overall, while still in early stages, Buffered looks like it could be making a spectacular splash in the VPN world so we’d definitely recommend keeping an eye on them.

    Disclaimer: I won a free year’s subscription through BestVPN’s Black Friday raffle.
    And a correction for Peter: Buffered does not currently have a forum.

    Now that I’ve had it for a couple of months and had time to use it, here’s my review of Buffered.

    PRICE: At $120 USD per year they are definitely not the cheapest out there, and I don’t really feel the impact of the price since I have a promotional subscription. There are cheaper VPN services that offer comparable feature sets for much less. However, Buffered does run promotions occasionally for 50% off or more.

    FEATURES: Buffered supports a wide range of devices, with a maximum of three simultaneous connections per account. I am quite impressed with the Windows client. It’s easy to use and contains some useful networking tools such as port discovery to help you find open ports when connected to a strange router.

    Buffered does not offer an Android app, but instead allows you to download an account-keyed OpenVPN configuration file for use with OpenVPN clients on Android. I recommend OpenVPN Connect, the open-source client. The downside of not having their own app is that you lose out on customization and the neat features that the desktop client offers.

    Buffered allows three simultaneous connections, which is reasonable (desktop, laptop, mobile device) for most users. However, there are cheaper services with higher or unlimited connection limits.

    They are continuously increasing their geographic coverage. When I first started using Buffered in December I was disappointed to see they didn’t have service in Japan. However, a Japanese connection was added one or two weeks before the writing of this review.

    RELIABILITY: Buffered is adequate for me, but this is where my experience with it suffered the most. Occasionally after long (2+ hours) use on public networks, VPN would sometimes encounter errors during use and drop the connection without attempting reestablishing itself. I haven’t been able to reliably reproduce this issue but it does happen infrequently and I need to disconnect and reconnect the client. It happens more often on Android. This could pose problems for people that require constant security over a long period of time. If possible I would like to be able to leave Buffered on all the time.

    SPEED: Wow, I was really impressed here. I’ve demo’d various VPN services such as AirVPN and others, and saw moderate to massive decrease in performance on my 100/10 line with Time Warner Cable. This would vary by protocol, but I never saw performance of higher than 60% on other services, free or otherwise. With Buffered, I typically only see around 10% loss of performance according to speed tests. It sometimes takes a while to connect to things, but once they start streaming I couldn’t notice a difference unless I was trying to max my line (e.g. Steam downloads).

    CUSTOMER SERVICE: Before and after I started using Buffered, I contacted their Support staff with questions using their online form. It’s too bad that they don’t have a 24/7 live support – response time was typically 24-48 hours. It also bears considering that Buffered operates out of an eastern European time zone.

    I found the quality of customer support to be high. The people that answered my emails were always polite and detailed in trying to answer my questions. On that note – a lot of these questions had to do with the fact that Buffered’s website is sometimes behind their actual policy. Specifically, their support page said unlimited connections while the front page said three, their payment page listed Discover as an accepted card despite that not being the case, and their web page showed a Japanese flag as an available server despite that not being true back in November 2014. Buffered promptly fixed the first two issues after I brought them to their attention.

    In summary, I have had a mostly great experience with Buffered so far. I would like to see increased reliability, but encryption is usually not a high-risk use for me so it did not bother me too much.
